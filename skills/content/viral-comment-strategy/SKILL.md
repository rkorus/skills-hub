# Viral Comment Strategy (LinkedIn)

**Source**: the original author's "Viral Comment Strategy" PDF from [your-domain.com] WordPress media library
**Purpose**: Generate high-performing LinkedIn comments that build visibility, followers, and inbound leads

## The Premise

Most people treat comments like reactions. High performers treat comments like **micro-content**.

A great comment does 3 things:
1. **Adds value** (a reframe, insight, or practical next step)
2. **Invites a reply** (from the author + others)
3. **Signals expertise** (so profile visitors convert into followers/DMs)

Goal: become the most useful person in the room, consistently.

## The 4 Patterns That Make Comments Pop

### 1. Start with a quote-worthy reframe (one line)

The "save/share" line. Should feel like a headline.

Templates:
- "___ is a skill, not a trait."
- "This isn't a ___ problem. It's a ___ problem."
- "Most people think ___. The real lever is ___."
- "The mistake is optimizing for ___. The win is optimizing for ___."

Rule: one sentence. No throat-clearing.

### 2. Add ONE fresh layer (not generic praise)

Never say "Great post." Add a layer that wasn't explicitly stated:
- A missing lever
- A second-order effect
- A simple framework
- A risk people ignore
- A measurement idea

Template: "What you wrote is true, and the part most teams miss is ___."

### 3. Make it actionable in one move

One practical step, not an essay.

Templates:
- "If you do this again, try ___."
- "The simplest next test is ___."
- "A clean way to operationalize this is ___."

### 4. End with an easy-to-answer question

The question turns a "nice comment" into a reply thread. Best format: give options.

- "Curious, is the constraint ___, ___, or ___?"
- "Which matters more here: ___ or ___?"
- "Are you optimizing for ___, ___, or ___?"

Rule: make it answerable in 5 seconds.

## Universal Comment Structure (Default Template)

```
Line 1: Reframe (quote-worthy)
Line 2-3: One insight layer
Line 4: One practical move / metric
Line 5: Easy question (with options)
```

Example:
```
This is the difference between activity and compounding.
Most teams do the loud part and skip the part that teaches behavior.
If you want it to stick, build one repeatable ritual around it.
Curious... what's the north star here: retention, repeat usage, or shareable moments?
```

## 5 Comment Types to Rotate

Rotate across the week to avoid sounding repetitive:

1. **The Reframe** - One-liner + question
2. **The Operator** - One practical tweak + "how are you measuring?"
3. **The Pattern Spotter** - Connect to 2-3 examples + "what's the common thread?"
4. **The Translator** - Turn their idea into a simple framework (3-part model, checklist)
5. **The Multiplier** - Suggest the "next iteration" they could run

## How to Pick Posts That Deliver Reach

Best posts to comment on:
- Posted within the last 60-180 minutes
- Already has high comment velocity (active conversation)
- Author's audience matches your ideal buyers/partners

Best people to comment on:
- Category leaders (big audience)
- Rising operators (fast-growing, high engagement)
- Your ICP's "trusted voices" (people your buyers read)

## The "Early or Best" Rule

Two ways to win:
- **Be early** on a fast-growing post
- **Be the best comment** (mini-framework / useful metric) if you're late

If late, don't do short praise. Drop a mini-asset.

PRO TIP: Turn on bell notifications for ICP/niche profiles. Comment as soon as they post.

## The 15-Minute Daily System

Daily targets:
- **2 big swings**: high-velocity posts in your niche
- **3 right rooms**: posts your ICP is likely reading
- **1 relationship**: comment on someone you want to know (peer/partner)

Consistency beats intensity.

## Engagement Rules

- Like 2-3 other good comments (signals participation)
- If someone replies to you: reply back within 1-3 hours
- Ask follow-up questions to keep the thread alive
- Keep comments readable: short lines, whitespace, no jargon, no walls of text

## The Biggest Mistake

Writing comments that try to impress instead of help.
"This is great." does nothing.

Winning mindset: "How do I make this post better for the reader?"

## Optional Soft CTAs (After Consistent Value)

- "If helpful, I can share the checklist I use for this."
- "Happy to DM a quick framework if you want it."
- "If you want, I can send the template."

## Aether's Traveling Comment Formula (Team Edition Playbook)

Source: LinkedIn Operations Playbook v1.0, Aether / dept-marketing-advertising
Proven: 185,262 profile appearances in 7 days. 98.6% from comments, not posts.

### Three-Part Structure

**Part 1: Name the Pattern** -- What the audience FEELS but has not articulated. One quotable line they want to repeat.

**Part 2: Add the Missing Layer** -- Prove you read the post. Build BEYOND it.
- A framework that explains WHY
- A counterpoint that adds nuance
- A practical example from real experience

**Part 3: Ask a Smart Question** -- Every reply resurfaces your comment.
- Options-based: "Optimize for A, B, or C?"
- Answerable in 5 seconds

### Human Voice Rules

**Do:**
- "Here's what I keep seeing..." (not "A key observation...")
- Reference exact details from the post
- Take a stance: "Most companies get this wrong by..."
- Short sentences. Punchy. Like texting a colleague.
- "I watched a client try this and..."
- Fragments and colloquial phrases. Real people do not write perfect prose.

**AI Tells (Avoid All):**
- Starts with "Great post!" or "Excellent insight!"
- Words: leverage, synergy, paradigm, holistic, delve, tapestry
- Every sentence the same length
- No specific details from the actual post
- Hedging: "It's worth considering..." "One might argue..."
- Numbered lists of 5+ points
- Ends with "Thanks for sharing!" or "Looking forward to more!"
- Overly polished prose with no personality

### Em Dash Prohibition (CRITICAL)

NEVER use em dashes. The number one AI writing tell on LinkedIn.

| Instead of Em Dash | Use This |
|-------------------|----------|
| Natural pause | Comma: "The real problem, most people miss it, is context" |
| Introduce a point | Colon: "Here's what nobody talks about: the memory layer" |
| Dramatic pause | Ellipsis: "Then week two hits... and the AI has amnesia" |
| Punchy emphasis | Period: "The agent forgot everything. Every customer. Every decision." |
| Complex clause | Restructure the sentence entirely |

### Reaction Selection

NEVER use "Like" (invisible in feed). Use:
- **Insightful** -- data-driven or analytical posts
- **Celebrate** -- achievement announcements, milestones
- **Love** -- personal stories, values-driven content
- **Support** -- vulnerability, challenges, setbacks
- **Funny** -- genuinely humorous content (rare)

### Target Tiers

| Tier | Window | Size | Examples |
|------|--------|------|---------|
| Tier 1 | Within 30 min | 500K+ | Sam Altman, Reid Hoffman, Ethan Mollick |
| Tier 2 | Within 2 hrs | 50K-500K | Bernard Marr, Allie K. Miller |
| Tier 3 | Within 4 hrs | Any | Clients, prospects, recent engagers |

### Comment Checklist
- [ ] Three-part structure (Pattern + Missing Layer + Smart Question)
- [ ] References SPECIFIC details from the post
- [ ] Sounds like the account holder texting a colleague
- [ ] Under 100 words
- [ ] Non-Like reaction selected
- [ ] Zero em dashes
- [ ] Zero AI tells
- [ ] Zero generic praise

### Traveling Comment Examples

**On AI agents failing:**
> Most agent failures are not about the model. They are about memory.
> The agent performs brilliantly in testing because context is fresh. Then week two hits... and the AI has amnesia. Every customer conversation, every preference, every decision, gone.
> Curious: are you seeing more failures from context loss or from bad prompting?

**On marketing automation:**
> The gap nobody talks about: automation creates consistency, but consistency without personality is spam.
> I watched a client automate 50 touchpoints and their reply rate dropped 40%. The emails were technically perfect. They just sounded like a robot wrote them. Because one did.
> What is your ratio of automated vs. personally written touchpoints?

## Integrated Rules from Aether's LinkedIn Strategy (Apr 2026)

### Post-Type Filter
COMMENT ON: Strategic takes, industry analysis, hot takes, new hire announcements, product launches, case studies, data/trend posts
SKIP: Shoutouts, appreciation posts, friend announcements, "congrats" posts, birthday wishes, personal milestones (unless from a key target with strategic content)

### Timing Strategy
- **Pre-post warm-up**: Comment on 2-3 posts from your target list BEFORE publishing your own content (warms the algorithm)
- **Post-post amplification**: After publishing, comment on 2-3 more posts within 60 min (first hour determines distribution)
- **4 daily windows**: 8-9 AM, 11 AM-12 PM, 2-3 PM, 5-6 PM EST
- **Volume ramp**: Start at 5-8/day, scale to 15-20/day over 4 weeks

### The "Reusable Thinking" Principle
Useful comments are reusable thinking, not applause. Every comment should be something the account holder could expand into its own post later. If it's not worth expanding, it's not worth commenting.

### Reply Monitoring
When someone replies to your account holder's comment, reply back within 24 hours. This creates threaded engagement which the algorithm rewards heavily (comments weighted 15x more than likes).

### Single-Block Format Rule
ALL comments must be ONE continuous block of text. No paragraph breaks, no line breaks between thoughts. This feels more authentic and conversational than structured mini-posts.

- Themes: shelf strategy, packaging, brand activations, experiential marketing, distribution, CPG velocity
